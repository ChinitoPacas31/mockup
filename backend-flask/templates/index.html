Introduction to Incubation

Egg incubation is a scientific process that combines biology, technology, and meticulous care. This guide will take you from the basics to advanced techniques to maximize your hatch rates.

Key Fact

Artificial incubation allows for precise control of all process parameters, achieving hatching rates up to 20% higher than in natural conditions when done correctly.

Incubation Key Parameters

Controlling these three critical factors is essential to the success of your incubation:

Temperature

37.2°C - 38.5°C

The most critical parameter. Fluctuations greater than ±0.5°C can negatively affect embryonic development and hatching rates.

Use multiple thermometers to verify accuracy.

Humidity

45-60% (65-75% In hatching)

Controlling humidity prevents dehydration of the egg. Too much humidity can prevent gas exchange.

Measure with a calibrated hygrometer.

Flip

3-5 times/day

Prevents the embryo from attaching to the membranes. Must be stopped 3 days before hatching.

Ideal angle: 45° each turn

Step-by-Step Incubation Process

1

Initial Preparation

  • Select fertile eggs of good quality (uniform size, no cracks)
  • Store at 12-15°C and 75% humidity (maximum 7 days)
  • Clean and disinfect the incubator thoroughly.
  • Preheat the incubator 24 hours in advance.
2

During Incubation

  • Place eggs horizontally or pointy side down.
  • Maintain stable parameters according to species
  • Turn regularly (automatic or manual)
  • Perform egg candling at 7 and 14 days.
3

Hatching Phase (Lockdown)

¡Attention!

The last 3 days: stop turning and increase humidity to 65-75%. Do not open the incubator during this critical period.

  • Chicks begin to peck (piping) 24 hours before
  • Do not assist unless 24 hours have passed since the first pip.
  • Leave in the incubator until completely dry.

Common problems and solutions

Low Hatching Rate

Causes:

Incorrect temperature/humidity, insufficient turning, old or poorly stored eggs.

Solution:

Verify equipment calibration, follow storage protocols, use fresh eggs.

Weak Chicks

Causes:

Temperature too high, low humidity, poor ventilation.

Solution:

Ensure adequate ventilation, maintain stable parameters, do not open the incubator unnecessarily.

Late hatching

Causes:

Very low temperature, eggs stored for too long.

Solution:

Check thermometer calibration; do not store eggs for more than 7 days before incubating.
